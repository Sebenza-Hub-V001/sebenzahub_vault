# Chapter 4.5 — How Sebenza Hub charges you

Before you start importing candidates, posting jobs, and running AI screening, take five minutes to understand the commercial model. Sebenza Hub is not free, and the way you'll pay is a combination of two things: a **subscription plan** and **per-feature metering**. Knowing this upfront will save you the surprise of hitting a paywall in the middle of a sprint.

This is a short, optional chapter — but if you're choosing a plan or trying to budget for the year, it's the most important read in Part 1.

## The two layers

Sebenza Hub charges in two layers, and most features are gated by *both*:

1. **Your subscription plan** sets the floor — what features are available to you at all, and how much of each metered feature you get included.
2. **Metering** sets the ceiling — many features (job posts, AI screening, video hours, WhatsApp messages) have monthly limits per plan. Go over the limit and you either upgrade or buy credits to top up.

Your plan is a flat monthly or annual fee. Metering is consumption-based on top of that.

## The recruiter plans

There are three tiers, each available monthly or annually (annual saves around 20%). All prices in **South African Rand (ZAR)**.

| Tier | Monthly | Annual (per month) | What you get |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | R0 | R0 | 3 job posts and basic candidate search. Useful for trying the platform before committing. |
| **Standard** | R999 | R799 | Professional recruiting with AI screening. The right starting point for a working solo recruiter or a small team running their first real desk on the platform. |
| **Premium** | R2,499 | R1,999 | Full platform access for agencies and teams. The right tier when you have multiple recruiters, multiple active mandates, and want every AI feature unlocked. |

There is no "Enterprise" tier in the standard plan list. If you need custom limits, white-label, or volume pricing, that's a direct conversation with Sebenza Hub — not a self-service plan.

> **Note on the Free tier.** It's a real product, not a trial. You can run it forever. But "3 job posts and basic candidate search" is genuinely the limit — no AI screening, no advanced sourcing, no analytics. It's enough to evaluate whether Sebenza Hub fits your workflow; it's not enough to run a real agency.

## What's metered (and what to watch)

These are the features that have monthly limits per plan, where you'll feel pressure first as you scale:

- **Job posts** — how many active jobs you can have published at once. Free is capped at 3.
- **AI interviews** — interview simulations or AI-driven screening conversations.
- **AI screening** — automated CV scoring, ranking, knockout-question evaluation.
- **AI coaching** — coaching prompts and recommendations the platform generates for you.
- **WhatsApp messages** — outbound WA messages sent via the integrated bot. WhatsApp Business API charges apply per template message; this is a real cost the platform passes through with metering.
- **Featured listings** — paid promotion of jobs to candidates.
- **Video interview hours** — recorded video interviews stored and analysed by the platform.
- **Active talent pools** — segmented candidate pools you can keep "live" at any time.
- **Active sequences** — automated email/message nurture sequences running concurrently.
- **API calls** — programmatic access to your data for integrations and exports.

The exact monthly limit per metered feature per plan is set by Sebenza Hub and may change over time. You can always see your current limits and current usage at **`/dashboard/recruiter/billing`** under the *Usage* section.

## Credits — the safety valve

When you bump into a metered limit, you have two choices:

1. **Upgrade your plan** for the rest of the billing period. This is the right move if you're regularly hitting the limit — you'll save money compared to topping up with credits every time.
2. **Buy credits** as a one-time top-up. Credits are spent against the same metered features. This is the right move if you've had an unusual month (a sudden client onboarding, a massive sourcing push, an audit) and don't want to commit to a higher plan permanently.

Credit purchase and history live at **`/dashboard/recruiter/billing`** under *Credits*. You can see your current balance, buy more, and review every credit transaction.

## What you DON'T pay extra for

Some things that look like they should be metered actually aren't — they're included in any paid plan:

- **Team members** — invite as many recruiters, managers, viewers, and admins as your plan allows. Plan tiers may include a team member ceiling, but invitations themselves don't cost credits.
- **Pipeline activity** — moving candidates through stages, scorecards, notes, reminders, scheduling. The hiring workflow is the platform; you don't pay per click.
- **Client management** — adding clients, logging calls, tracking engagements, managing contracts. The CRM side is included.
- **Analytics dashboards** — KPI Dashboard, predictive analytics, panel analysis, diversity reporting. Reading reports doesn't burn credits.
- **Compliance reporting** — POPIA exports, B-BBEE reports, EE reports, audit trail access. Compliance is core platform functionality, not a paid extra.

The pattern is: the *connective tissue* of running an agency is included in your plan. The *AI and bulk-action* features have metering on top.

## How to read your billing page

`/dashboard/recruiter/billing` is your dashboard for everything commercial. The sections you'll use most:

| Section | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Plan** | Your current tier, billing interval, next renewal date |
| **Payment methods** | Cards on file, set the default |
| **Invoices** | Download every invoice, with VAT for SA tax purposes |
| **Subscription history** | Every plan change, when and why |
| **Usage history** | Per-feature consumption against your monthly limits |
| **Spending summary** | Total spend (subscription + credits) over time |
| **Credits** | Current balance, purchase, transaction history |
| **ROI** | Sebenza Hub's calculation of placements made vs platform cost |
| **Tax summary** | South African VAT summary for accounting |
| **Plan recommendation** | AI suggestion of which plan fits your usage pattern |

The two pages you should bookmark from day one are **Usage history** (so you can see how close to a metered limit you are) and **Plan recommendation** (so you know when an upgrade would actually save you money vs the credits you're burning).

## Practical advice for picking a starting plan

- **Solo recruiter, evaluating the platform:** Start on **Free**. Post 3 jobs, see how the workflow feels, see how candidates respond. Don't commit until you've made at least one real submission.
- **Solo recruiter, working real mandates:** Start on **Standard (R999/mo)**. Take the annual discount only after 2–3 months on monthly — switching back is allowed but it's friction you don't need on day one.
- **Small team (2–5 recruiters), running multiple mandates:** Start on **Standard (R999/mo)**, watch the Usage history page closely, and upgrade to **Premium** the moment you start hitting limits twice in one month. The R2,499/mo Premium tier is roughly 2.5× the price of Standard but includes substantially more of every metered feature — once you're consistently at the limit, the credit top-ups will cost more than the upgrade.
- **Agency with 5+ recruiters, established book:** Start on **Premium (R2,499/mo)** annual. The annual discount alone (R500/month saved vs monthly billing) makes the commitment worth it.
- **Need things that aren't on the Premium feature list:** Talk to Sebenza Hub about a custom arrangement. Don't try to brute-force a Premium plan into doing something it isn't designed for.

## A note on payment

Sebenza Hub processes payments in **ZAR** through a South African payment gateway. You'll add a card or debit order at `/dashboard/recruiter/billing` → *Payment methods*. Invoices are issued automatically and are SARS-compliant for your tax records.

If you need to pause your subscription temporarily (e.g. during a slow month or a holiday shutdown), there's a **Pause subscription** option in the billing page — you don't have to cancel and re-sign-up.
