# Chapter 5 — Your agency profile

Your agency profile is the single most important thing you'll do in your first week. It's what clients see when they consider working with you. It's what feeds the AI scoring systems (Chapter 6). It's what fills your offer letters, your career site, your brand page. A half-finished profile reads as a half-serious agency, and clients respond accordingly.

This chapter walks through every section. The whole thing takes 30–60 minutes if you have your information ready; longer if you have to dig up your B-BBEE certificate or count up your placements. Don't try to fill it all in one sitting — work through the sections in order, save as you go, and come back tomorrow for the parts that need research.

## Where it lives

Open `/dashboard/recruiter/profile`. Everything in this chapter is on that one page, organised into expandable sections.

## The six sections

The profile has roughly 60 fields organised into six sections. Fill them in this order — earlier sections feed the impression a reader builds before they get to the later ones.

### 1. Branding (the part clients see first)

The visual top of your profile. This is what loads first when a client opens your page.

| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| **Agency logo** | A clean, square version of your logo. PNG with transparent background if you have one. |
| **Cover photo** | A wide banner image (team photo, office, abstract brand graphic). Avoid stock photos — they're spotted instantly. |
| **Agency name** | Already set in onboarding. Should match your registered name. |
| **Description** | Two to four sentences. What you do, who you do it for, what makes you different. Not a mission statement — a positioning statement. |
| **Founded year** | The year your agency started trading. Helps set credibility. |
| **Headquarters** | City, province, country. |
| **Agency size** | A range: 1–10, 10–50, 50–250, 250–1000, 1000+. Be honest — clients verify on LinkedIn anyway. |

### 2. Contact and web presence

| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| **Agency email** | The one clients should use, not your personal email. |
| **Agency phone** | A phone someone actually answers. |
| **Website** | Your live agency site. Leave blank if it's not ready — don't link a placeholder. |
| **LinkedIn** | Your agency's LinkedIn page. Setting this and getting it verified earns you a trust badge (Chapter 6). |
| **Twitter / Facebook / Instagram / YouTube / GitHub** | Optional. Add any that have actual recent activity. Empty links hurt more than missing ones. |

### 3. Credentials and track record

This is where you prove you're real and you've done the work.

- **Certifications** — APSO, IITPSA, professional bodies you belong to. Each entry takes a name, issuer, year, and optional expiry.
- **Awards** — agency awards you've won. Title, issuer, year.
- **Languages** — the languages you and your team can recruit in. Each entry takes a language and proficiency level.
- **Total placements** — your lifetime placement count. Be honest. Clients verify this against your LinkedIn and your testimonials.
- **Success rate** — percentage of submitted candidates that get hired. Industry benchmark is 15–25%.
- **Average time-to-fill** — days from job open to offer accepted. Industry benchmark varies by sector but 30–45 days is typical.
- **Average retention rate** — percentage of placed candidates still in role after one year. **This matters for your trust tier** — 85%+ earns you a badge automatically (Chapter 6).
- **Industries served** — broad sectors. Should match the sectors you picked during onboarding.
- **Sub-industries served** — more granular sub-sectors per industry, if you specialise.
- **Placement highlights** — three to five "name brand" placements. Role, company, year. No personal information about the candidate.

### 4. Commercial terms

Your fee structure and how you do business. Clients filter and shortlist on these fields.

- **Fee structure** — percentage, fixed/retainer, or hybrid.
- **Fee percentage min / max** — your typical range as a percentage of first-year salary. Most SA agencies sit in 15–25%.
- **Payment terms** — upfront, 50/50, on-placement, or net-30 after start.
- **Guarantee period** — how long you'll replace a placement free if they leave. 3 months is standard; 6 months is competitive.
- **Contract types** — permanent, contract, executive search, contract-to-permanent, RPO.

These can be overridden per client engagement (Chapter 9), but the values here are the defaults shown on your public profile.

### 5. Compliance (SA specific, mandatory)

This isn't optional in South Africa. Filling it in correctly is a competitive advantage; filling it in incorrectly is a legal risk.

- **B-BBEE level** — your certified level, 1 to 8. Setting this and uploading the document earns you a trust badge.
- **B-BBEE expiry date** — when your current certificate expires. Set a reminder.
- **POPIA compliant** — flip to true once you've signed the platform's POPIA undertaking.
- **Registration number** — your CIPC registration.
- **VAT number** — if you're VAT-registered.

Without B-BBEE level + document, you cannot reach Silver trust tier (Chapter 6) and you'll be filtered out of any client search that requires a B-BBEE level.

### 6. Narrative content (the part that actually persuades)

The numbers above prove you exist. The narrative below proves you're worth hiring.

- **Video introduction** — a 60–90 second video of you (or the principal of the agency) explaining what you do and who you do it for. Sebenza Hub's AI analyses tone and talking points and gives you feedback (Chapter 6).
- **Case studies** — challenge → approach → result, in plain language. Three case studies beats thirty bullet points.
- **Testimonials** — quotes from real clients with their name, company, and a star rating. **Three testimonials earns you the `client_endorsed` badge automatically** (Chapter 6). The smart testimonial engine can request these for you — you don't have to chase clients personally.

## The AI Profile Coach

As you fill in sections, the **AI Profile Coach** generates suggestions in the right-hand panel. It catches:

- Empty critical fields ("you haven't added a description")
- Weak descriptions ("your description is generic — try mentioning a specific sector")
- Missing competitive context ("you haven't added any case studies — agencies with case studies get 2.3× more inbound enquiries")
- Compliance gaps ("B-BBEE document missing — your trust tier is capped at Bronze until you add it")

Take the suggestions seriously. They're based on what high-performing agencies on the platform actually do.

## What you can save vs what's required

Almost every field can be saved blank and revisited. The minimum to get a usable public profile is:

- Logo
- Description
- At least one sector
- At least one industry served
- Fee structure
- Either B-BBEE level + document OR a note that B-BBEE certification is in progress

Everything else is competitive — the more you fill in, the better you look. But the minimums above will get you out of the "obviously incomplete" zone where clients won't even consider you.

## Pro tips

- **Don't lie about stats.** Total placements, success rate, retention rate — these all surface as trust badges (Chapter 6). The trust system audits them against your actual platform activity over time. Inflated stats today make you look bad in six months.
- **Update quarterly.** Total placements should grow. Time-to-fill should improve. If your profile shows the same numbers in October as it did in April, you look stagnant.
- **Get the video done early.** Filming a 60-second video feels like overkill on day one. It's not. Agencies with intro videos get noticeably more inbound interest.
- **Request testimonials proactively.** The smart testimonial engine sends requests for you. Use it. Three testimonials is the magic number for the badge.
- **Profile visibility matters.** Make sure `profileVisibility` is set to **public** unless you have a specific reason to hide. A private profile cannot be discovered by clients searching the platform.

- [ ] POPIA compliance flag set
