# Chapter 6 — AI profile assets: DNA Card, Reputation Score, Trust Tier

When a client lands on your agency profile, they don't read every word. They scan for credibility signals — badges, scores, percentile rankings, capacity indicators. Sebenza Hub generates these signals automatically from your profile data and your platform activity. This chapter explains what each one means, how it's calculated, and — crucially — exactly how to move yours up.

There are five AI-generated assets on your profile. The most actionable is the **Trust Tier**, so we'll start there.

## 1. Trust Tier — Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum

This is the badge that appears most prominently next to your agency name. There are four levels: **Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum**. Every new agency starts at Bronze. You move up by earning **verification badges**.

### How it works

There are **7 verification badges** on every recruiter profile. Your trust tier is calculated from the count:

| Badges earned | Score | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 out of 7 | 0–39 | 🥉 **Bronze** |
| 3–4 out of 7 | 40–64 | 🥈 **Silver** |
| 5 out of 7 | 65–84 | 🥇 **Gold** |
| 6–7 out of 7 | 85–100 | 💎 **Platinum** |

The score is recalculated **every time your profile is viewed** — it's not a snapshot. The moment you earn a new badge, your tier updates immediately.

### The 7 badges and how to earn them

Six of the seven badges auto-earn from data already in your profile. You don't have to ask for them — fill in the right field and the badge appears.

| # | Badge | How to earn it | Where the data comes from |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **🆔 Identity Verified** | Verify your email address | Your signup email — automatic on first login |
| 2 | **🔗 LinkedIn Verified** | Link and verify your agency LinkedIn | Profile → Contact section → LinkedIn URL + verification |
| 3 | **🇿🇦 B-BBEE Certified** | Add your B-BBEE level **and** upload your certificate URL | Profile → Compliance → both fields required |
| 4 | **⭐ Client Endorsed** | Collect 3 or more client testimonials | Profile → Narrative → Testimonials (use the smart testimonial engine to request them) |
| 5 | **🏆 Placement Proven** | Reach 10 or more recorded placements | Auto-tracked from your placements activity |
| 6 | **💚 Retention Star** | Achieve 85% or higher average retention rate | Auto-calculated from your placement records |
| 7 | **⚡ Response Champion** | Maintain fast and consistent responsiveness across the platform | Awarded based on your inbox responsiveness signals |

The first six are the ones you can directly control. The seventh (Response Champion) is awarded based on your in-platform responsiveness — answering candidate and client messages within target windows. There's no "claim" button for it; just stay responsive.

### The Bronze → Platinum playbook

If you're starting from zero today, here's the fastest path:

1. **Day 1 → Bronze (0 badges → 1 badge):** Verify your email. Done. (Identity Verified)
2. **Week 1 → Silver (1 → 3 badges):** Add your LinkedIn URL and verify. Add your B-BBEE level and upload your certificate URL. You now have 3 badges and you're Silver.
3. **Month 1 → Gold (3 → 5 badges):** Use the smart testimonial engine to request testimonials from your three best existing clients. Once 3 land, you're at 4 badges. Stay responsive in the Unified Inbox (Chapter 22) for two weeks of consistent activity to earn Response Champion. That's 5 badges = Gold.
4. **First year → Platinum (5 → 6+ badges):** Reach 10 platform-recorded placements (Placement Proven). Hit 85%+ retention on those placements (Retention Star). That's 7 badges = Platinum.

The honest truth: **Platinum is reserved for agencies with real volume on the platform.** You can't buy it, you can't fake it, and you can't game it. Bronze through Gold are achievable in the first month if you're diligent. Platinum takes a year of consistent placements.

### Why trust tier matters commercially

When clients filter the agency directory, they can require a minimum trust tier. A client looking for a "Gold or above" recruiter literally cannot see your profile if you're Silver. That's the leverage. The tier isn't decorative — it's a gate.

## 2. DNA Card

The **DNA Card** is a four-axis radar showing your agency's strengths in plain language. Each axis is scored 0–100.

| Axis | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Speed** | How quickly you fill roles compared to platform average. Driven by your average time-to-fill. |
| **Quality** | How successful your placements are. Driven by submission-to-hire conversion and offer-acceptance rate. |
| **Diversity** | The demographic breadth of your placements. Driven by your diversity analytics across pipeline stages. |
| **Retention** | How long your placed candidates stay. Driven by your average retention rate. |

The DNA Card is computed monthly from your placement records. It's the asset clients use to pick a recruiter for a specific kind of role: a fast-moving startup looks for high Speed, a regulated industry looks for high Quality and Diversity, a conservative employer looks for high Retention.

You don't earn the DNA Card by claiming it — it generates from your activity. To improve a low score on any axis, focus on the underlying metric (close jobs faster, place more carefully, broaden your sourcing, or follow up on retention).

## 3. Reputation Score

The **Reputation Score** is a single number from 0–100 showing your overall standing. It's the average of four sub-scores stored on your profile:

| Sub-score | What it captures |
|---|---|
| **Candidate NPS** | Net Promoter Score from candidates you've worked with — collected via post-interview and post-placement surveys |
| **Client Satisfaction** | Direct CSAT scores from your corporate clients |
| **Placement Retention** | Same data as the Retention DNA axis — how long candidates stay |
| **Responsiveness** | How quickly you respond to platform messages, candidate communications, and client requests |

The Reputation Score is what shows up next to your name in inbound matching results. A high reputation score puts you near the top of any search a client runs on the platform. A score below 60 deprioritises you significantly.

To improve it: chase your candidate NPS surveys (Chapter 30), respond to messages faster (Chapter 22), and run honest exit conversations with placed candidates so you catch retention issues before they become numbers.

## 4. Competitive Benchmarking

The **Competitive Benchmarking** asset shows your percentile ranking versus other recruiters in the same sector. It's not a number you control directly — it's a relative comparison.

If you're in the **fintech sector** and your time-to-fill is faster than 73% of fintech recruiters on the platform, you'll see "Top 27% — Fintech, Time-to-Fill" on your profile. Each sector has its own ranking, so an agency strong in fintech might be unranked in mining and that's fine.

The benchmarking asset is **only generated when you ask for it** via Profile → Generate Benchmark. It's not automatic. Run it monthly to keep it fresh; clients judge benchmarks older than 90 days as stale.

## 5. Capacity Signal

The **Capacity Signal** is the simplest asset and the most overlooked. It's a per-sector indicator that says one of three things:

- 🟢 **Available** — you have bandwidth to take on new mandates in this sector
- 🟡 **Limited** — you're busy but can take on selective new work
- 🔴 **At Capacity** — you cannot take on new mandates in this sector right now

You set this manually per sector. **Use it.** Clients respect agencies that flip to "At Capacity" when they're genuinely full — it signals discipline and stops them from approaching you with work you can't service well. Pretending to be Available when you're drowning leads to dropped balls and reputation damage.

A good rhythm: review your Capacity Signal at the start of every week. If you're carrying more than 8–10 active mandates in a sector, flip to Limited or At Capacity. When something closes, flip back.

## How clients actually see these

When a client opens the agency directory and clicks your profile, the layout (top to bottom) is roughly:

1. **Logo and agency name** — with the **Trust Tier badge** prominently next to it
2. **Reputation Score** as a single big number
3. **Capacity Signal** per sector (so they know if it's worth contacting you)
4. **DNA Card** radar chart
5. **Competitive Benchmarking** percentiles (if generated)
6. **The narrative content** — description, case studies, testimonials, video

The first three things they see are all AI-generated. Spending an hour on your description while ignoring your trust tier badges is the wrong priority order. **Earn the badges first; polish the narrative second.**

## Checklist

- [ ] Trust tier is at least **Silver** (LinkedIn verified + B-BBEE certificate uploaded)
- [ ] All seven possible badges have been reviewed; you know which ones you're missing and why
- [ ] DNA Card has been generated at least once (visit Profile → Generate)
- [ ] Reputation Score is visible on your profile
- [ ] Capacity Signal is set per sector and you have a weekly habit of updating it
- [ ] Competitive Benchmarking has been run for your primary sector
