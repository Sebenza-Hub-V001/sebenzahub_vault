# Chapter 3 — Onboarding: the 6 things you need to fill in

After you click your magic link for the first time, Sebenza Hub takes you to **/onboarding**. This is where the platform learns who you are and what kind of work you're looking for. It also asks for the legal consent it needs under POPIA.

Onboarding takes about five minutes if you have your details to hand. You don't need to be perfect — you can refine everything later — but the answers you give here drive your job matches and your dashboard, so it's worth doing thoughtfully rather than rushing it.

## Step 1 — Choose your role

The first screen asks you to pick your role:

- **Individual** — job seeker (this guide)
- **Recruiter** — talent agency
- **Business** — employer
- **Admin** — platform operator (almost certainly not you)

Pick **Individual**.

> ⚠️ **Your role is locked after this screen.** You cannot switch from Individual to Recruiter (or any other role) on the same account later. If you need both — for example, you're a job seeker who later starts a small recruitment agency — you'll need a separate account with a different email. Make sure Individual is what you want before moving on.

## Step 2 — Fill in the onboarding form

The Individual onboarding form asks for six things. Here they are, with the reason each one matters.

### 1. Your name

First and last name. This appears on your CVs, your applications, and your public profile (if you make it public). Use your real, professional name — the one you'd put on a contract.

### 2. Your location

**Province and city are both required.** Sebenza Hub uses your location to filter job matches and to surface salary data relevant to where you actually live. If you're prepared to relocate, you can tune that later in Settings — for now, put down where you are *today*.

### 3. Your phone number

Your phone is used for WhatsApp and SMS notifications if you opt in to them later. Recruiters can also reach you here if you allow it. Put down a number you actually answer.

### 4. Your job title and experience level

You can pick from a list of more than 300 standard job titles, or type a custom one if your role isn't on the list. Pick the title that best matches what you're looking for *next*, not necessarily what you currently do — Sebenza Hub uses this to drive job recommendations.

Then pick your experience level:

- **Entry** — fresh graduate, first job, junior
- **Intermediate** — a few years of relevant experience
- **Senior** — established in your field, often leading work
- **Executive** — leading teams, departments, or organisations

If you're between two levels, pick the lower one. Recruiters filter jobs by level, and you'd rather see a slightly easier role than miss the harder one.

### 5. Your skills

Add between 1 and 10 skills, each with a proficiency level. Don't dump every skill you've ever touched — pick the ones that are actually relevant to the work you want next, and rate them honestly. You can always add more after onboarding in [[16-how-tos/individual/07-skills-credentials-video|Chapter 7]].

A practical tip: rate yourself the way you'd want a recruiter to interpret you, not the way you feel on a bad day. "Intermediate" is the most common honest answer for skills you use regularly but aren't a guru in.

### 6. Profile visibility

You can make your profile **public** (recruiters can find and contact you) or **private** (you can only apply to jobs yourself; recruiters cannot search for you).

Public is usually the right answer if you're actively looking — being findable is half the point. Make it private if you're employed and don't want your current employer to stumble across your profile while sourcing candidates. You can change this any time in Settings.

## Step 3 — POPIA consent

Before you can finish onboarding, Sebenza Hub asks you to **tick a POPIA consent box.** This is required by South African law (the Protection of Personal Information Act). By ticking it, you're agreeing that Sebenza Hub can:

- Store the personal information you've just provided
- Use it to match you to jobs
- Share relevant parts of it with recruiters and employers when you apply
- Process it for the purposes the platform exists to serve

You **must** consent to use the platform — there is no Individual experience without it. The good news is that POPIA also gives you rights: you can review what's stored, correct it, or ask for it to be deleted at any time. Chapter 8 covers your privacy rights in detail.

If anything in the consent text is unclear, read it before you tick. Don't tick boxes you don't understand.

## Step 4 — Submit

When you click submit, Sebenza Hub saves your profile and redirects you to **/dashboard/individual/profile**. That's your new home base. Chapter 4 walks you through it.

## What happens if you make a mistake

Almost everything you entered on the onboarding form can be edited later in your profile or settings — name, phone, location, skills, job title, experience level, visibility. The only exception is your **role**, which is locked.

So don't agonise over the form. Get it close to right, click submit, and tune it after you've seen the dashboard.
