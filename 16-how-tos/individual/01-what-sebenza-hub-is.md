# Chapter 1 — What Sebenza Hub is and who it's for

Sebenza Hub is a South African recruitment and career platform. As an **Individual** — a job seeker — you use it to find work, apply to jobs, prepare for interviews, develop your skills, and manage your career over time. It's the place you live while you're looking, not just a job board you visit and leave.

## What makes it different from a traditional job board

Most job boards do one thing: they show you listings. Sebenza Hub does that too, but the listings are only the starting point. The platform is built around the idea that finding a job is a *project* — and projects need profiles, applications, tracking, preparation, learning, and follow-through. All of that lives in one place.

Specifically, as an Individual you get:

- **A complete profile** that recruiters and employers can find you through. Your CV is just one part of it; you also have skills, credentials, a video introduction, and a portfolio.
- **Three ways to find jobs**: browse the full market, let AI match you automatically, or run precise searches with your own filters. Listings come from jobs posted directly on Sebenza Hub *and* from external aggregators (Adzuna, CareerJet), so you see more of the market in one place.
- **An application tracker** so you always know where each application stands — who's read it, who's interviewing you, and who's gone quiet.
- **AI tools** that activate when you need them: CV review, mock interviews, salary negotiation, career coaching.
- **Learning paths and skill verification** for when the job you want needs skills you don't have yet.
- **A community** of other job seekers, mentors, and shared interview experiences.

## Who this guide is for

You should be reading this if:

- You're job-hunting (actively or passively) in South Africa.
- You've signed up for a Sebenza Hub Individual account, or you're about to.
- You want to use the platform properly instead of clicking around hoping to figure it out.

You don't need any technical background. You don't need to read every chapter — but if you're brand new, the first four chapters (Part 1) take you from "I just heard of this" to "I know what I'm looking at on the dashboard." That's a useful place to be before you start customising anything.

## Three things to know before you continue

1. **Sebenza Hub is built around South African compliance** — POPIA (privacy), B-BBEE, and Employment Equity. You'll be asked for consent during onboarding. This is normal and required by law; Chapter 8 explains what you're consenting to.
2. **There are several user types on the platform** — Individuals (you), Recruiters (talent agencies), Businesses (employers), and Admins (the team running the platform). This guide is only about the Individual experience. The dashboard you see and the routes you visit are different from what other user types see.
3. **Your role is locked after onboarding.** Once you choose "Individual" during signup, you can't switch to a Recruiter or Business account on the same login. If you need a different role later, you'll need a separate account. Chapter 3 explains why this matters before you click anything.

## What you'll be able to do by the end of this guide

By the time you finish Part 4 of this guide, you will have:

- Created an account and completed onboarding.
- Built a profile that recruiters can actually find and respond to.
- Found jobs worth applying to, applied properly, and tracked the results.
- Prepared for interviews and responded to offers like someone who knows what they're doing.

Parts 5 and 6 are there for when something goes wrong, or when you want to use the platform's more advanced features.
