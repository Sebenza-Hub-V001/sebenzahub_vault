# Chapter 10 — Saved searches and favourites

Two small features that punch above their weight. Used together, they turn job hunting from a constant scroll into a structured process. This chapter takes about five minutes to read and ten to set up — but it'll save you hours over the next few months.

## The difference between saved searches and favourites

These get confused all the time. They are not the same thing.

| Feature | What it does | When to use it |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Saved Search** | Saves a *set of filters* so you can re-run them or get alerts on new matches | When you want jobs to come *to you* on an ongoing basis |
| **Favourites** | Bookmarks a *specific job* so you can come back to it | When you want to compare jobs side by side or apply later |

In short: a saved search is a query that finds jobs in the future. A favourite is a single job you want to remember today.

Use both. They serve different purposes.

## Saved Searches

Go to **Saved Searches** (`/dashboard/individual/saved-searches`) to manage them. To create one, run a search you like in **Manual Search** (Chapter 9), then save the criteria.

### Why saved searches are valuable

Most people log in, scroll through whatever's new, get distracted, and miss good roles that quietly went up overnight. Saved searches invert that: instead of you remembering to look, the platform checks for you and tells you when something matches.

This matters because the best roles often disappear within days. If you're not on the alert list, by the time you wander back into Auto Match the job is already gone.

### How to set them up well

Aim for **two or three saved searches**, not ten. Each one should answer a specific question:

- **Saved Search 1** — your default (broad-coverage). Loose filters: your role, your city, your salary floor. Daily digest.
- **Saved Search 2** — a stretch search. Slightly senior to where you are, or in a related role you'd be willing to pivot to. Daily digest.
- **Saved Search 3** *(optional)* — a passion search. A specific company you want to work for, or a role type you'd jump at if it appeared. Real-time alert.

If you have more than five saved searches, you'll either drown in alerts or start ignoring them. Both make the feature useless.

### Alert frequency

Each saved search has an alert setting:

| Frequency | When to use it |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Real-time** | Only for very specific, high-value searches (a particular company, a rare role) |
| **Daily digest** | The default. One email per day with all new matches across the search. |
| **Weekly digest** | For passive searches you want to keep an eye on without urgency. |
| **None** | When you want to keep the saved filters but don't want to be notified — you just re-run them manually. |

### Tuning over time

After two weeks, look at your saved searches. For each one:

- Are the alerts useful, or are most of them irrelevant? (Tighten the filters.)
- Are you getting too few alerts? (Loosen the filters or broaden the role.)
- Is one search dominating your inbox while another is silent? (Adjust frequencies.)

Saved searches are not set-and-forget. They get better as you tune them.

## Favourites

Go to **Favourites** (`/dashboard/individual/favourites`) to see your bookmarked jobs. To add one, click the favourite icon (usually a star or heart) on any job listing.

### Why bookmark instead of applying immediately

Three reasons:

1. **Comparison.** When you have 5–10 favourites in one place, you can compare them side by side and pick the ones genuinely worth your effort. Applying to the first thing you see is usually a mistake.
2. **Batching.** Applications take time and energy. Bookmarking lets you collect candidates throughout the week and then have a focused application session — better quality, less context-switching.
3. **Cooling off.** Sometimes a job looks great when you find it and obviously wrong an hour later. Favourites give you that hour.

### How to use Favourites well

- **Triage often.** When you bookmark, you're saying "this is interesting." When you sit down to apply, prune ruthlessly — remove the ones that don't survive a second look.
- **Use it for comparison, not as a wishlist.** Favourites is not a place where 50 jobs sit forever. If a job has been in your favourites for a month and you haven't applied, you weren't going to.
- **Apply in a focused block.** Set aside 60–90 minutes once or twice a week and process your favourites. Better than five-minute spurts every day.

## A weekly rhythm that works

Once you've set up saved searches and started using favourites, your job hunt has a rhythm:

- **Daily** — Open the digest emails from your saved searches. Spend two minutes scanning. Add anything promising to favourites. Don't apply yet.
- **Mid-week** — Open Auto Match for ten minutes. Add anything good to favourites.
- **Weekly application session** (60–90 minutes) — Open Favourites. Triage. Apply to the top three to five with care (Chapter 11). Remove the rest from Favourites — if you didn't apply, you weren't going to.
- **Every two weeks** — Tune your saved searches. Adjust frequencies, tighten or loosen filters.

This rhythm beats scrolling-as-job-hunt by a wide margin. It produces fewer applications but better ones, and it's sustainable — you can run it for months without burning out.

## Common mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Twenty saved searches, all with real-time alerts | Cut to three. Use daily digest. |
| Hundreds of favourites accumulated, never applied to | Triage weekly. Be willing to remove. |
| Applying directly from search results without favouriting first | Bookmark, then apply in batches. The batched applications will be better. |
| Saved search filters too broad (all jobs in your city) | Add a salary floor or a role keyword to cut the noise. |
| Saved search filters too narrow (one job title in one suburb) | Loosen one filter at a time until you get a useful match volume. |

## Checklist

- [ ] Created at least one saved search with alerts enabled
- [ ] Set a sensible alert frequency (probably daily digest, not real-time)
- [ ] Favourited at least 5 jobs you intend to apply to
- [ ] Decided on a weekly application session (which day, what time)
- [ ] You understand the difference between a saved search and a favourite
