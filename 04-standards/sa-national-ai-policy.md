# South Africa National AI Policy (Draft)

## Overview

The **Draft South Africa National AI Policy** was published in Government Gazette No. 54477 on 10 April 2026, having been approved by Cabinet on 25 March 2026. It is currently a **draft policy framework** — not yet legislation — but signals South Africa's regulatory direction for AI governance.

**Public comments close: 10 June 2026.**

## Risk-Based Classification

The policy adopts a risk-based approach inspired by the **EU AI Act**, classifying AI systems into four tiers:

| Risk Level | Description | Regulatory Burden |
|---|---|---|
| **Unacceptable** | AI systems posing clear threats to safety, livelihoods, or rights | Banned outright |
| **High** | AI in critical domains: employment, credit, law enforcement, education | Bias audits, explainability, HITL, impact assessments, potential certification |
| **Medium** | Advisory or semi-automated systems | Transparency notices, accountability logging |
| **Low** | Internal operational AI | Standard compliance |

**Recruitment and hiring AI is explicitly classified as HIGH risk** — making Sebenza Hub's core AI features (Predictive Hiring, Job Matching, Battle Cards, Assessment Scoring) subject to the strictest regulatory requirements.

## Six Principles of Responsible AI

| # | Principle | Core Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Fairness** | Diverse training data, bias testing, gender/human rights impact assessments, independent certification for high-stakes AI |
| 2 | **Reliability & Safety** | Consistent operation, safety brakes, human override capability |
| 3 | **Privacy & Security** | Align with POPIA's 8 conditions, data protection by design, data minimisation, Privacy Impact Assessments |
| 4 | **Inclusiveness** | Disability inclusion, child data ethics, accessibility for all South Africans, address historical disparities |
| 5 | **Transparency** | Sufficient explainability, plain-language AI notices, right to challenge automated decisions, POPIA Section 71 compliance, algorithm impact assessments |
| 6 | **Accountability** | Named accountable official, organisational responsibility for AI outcomes, clear escalation paths |

## Implementation Timeline

| Phase | Period | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Year 1** | 2025/26 | Finalise policy, publish draft requirements for unacceptable risks |
| **Year 2** | 2026/27 | Implement high-risk regulations, develop sector strategies |
| **Year 3** | 2027/28 | Full implementation across all risk levels |

**For Sebenza Hub:** High-risk regulations expected in Year 2 (2026/27), giving approximately **12–18 months** from policy finalisation to achieve compliance.

## POPIA Section 71 — Automated Decision-Making

The policy explicitly references POPIA Section 71, which requires:

1. **Notification** — Data subjects must be informed when automated decisions materially affect them
2. **Representations** — Data subjects must have the opportunity to make representations (i.e., challenge the decision)
3. **Review** — Automated decisions must be subject to human review on request

This is directly relevant to Sebenza Hub's Predictive Hiring (hire/no-hire verdicts), Job Matching (candidate scoring), and Assessment Scoring.

## Sebenza Hub Compliance Status

See [[09-sources/sa-ai-policy-compliance-review-2026-04-11]] for the full gap analysis.

**Summary:** Sebenza Hub has foundational compliance infrastructure (POPIA consent, audit trails, AI logging, bias audit tables) but significant gaps in:
- **Candidate-facing transparency** — no AI disclosure notices, no explainability, no appeal mechanism
- **Bias auditing operationalisation** — infrastructure exists but isn't running
- **AI-specific consent** — general consent doesn't cover AI processing
- **Accountability** — no designated AI officer, no incident response procedures
- **Human oversight** — automation engine lacks approval gates, no global kill-switch
