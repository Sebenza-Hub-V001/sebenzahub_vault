# Source: SEO Plan

**Raw file:** `raw/articles/seo-plan.md`
**Ingested:** 2026-04-07

## Summary

A comprehensive SEO strategy document for Sebenza Hub covering technical SEO, on-page optimization, keyword strategy, content strategy, local SEO, link building, structured data, and an implementation roadmap. Targets the South African recruitment market with a goal of 10,000+ monthly organic visitors within 12 months.

## Key Claims

### Current SEO State (March 2026)
- Per-page dynamic meta tags via `react-helmet-async` on 35+ pages
- Full `robots.txt` with crawl rules and `sitemap.xml` (static + dynamic pages)
- Open Graph + Twitter Card tags on all public pages
- 8 JSON-LD structured data schemas: Organization, LocalBusiness, WebSite, SoftwareApp, FAQ, Breadcrumb, JobPosting, Article
- Bot meta injection middleware for 13 static pages + dynamic routes
- Public job listing pages with live search and province/industry landing pages
- PWA with offline support, Core Web Vitals tracking reported to GA4
- Blog platform with article schema and breadcrumbs

### Technical SEO Priorities
1. **SSR/Prerendering** — Critical: React SPA may not be fully indexed. Options: Rendertron (recommended), static site generation, or full Next.js migration (long-term)
2. **Image optimization** — WebP/AVIF with lazy loading
3. **URL structure** — Minor improvements suggested (e.g., `/p/:userId` → `/profile/:slug`)

### Keyword Strategy
- **Primary keywords**: "recruitment software south africa" (500-1K/mo), "job search south africa" (5K-10K/mo), "ATS south africa" (200-500/mo)
- **Content pillars**: Recruitment in SA, AI in hiring, career development, recruiter best practices, HR compliance & legal
- **Content calendar**: 8 articles/month for first 3 months, targeting long-tail SA-specific terms

### Goals
1. Page 1 rankings for key SA recruitment terms within 6-12 months
2. 10,000+ monthly organic visitors within 12 months
3. Topical authority in SA recruitment, HR tech, AI hiring
4. Qualified leads through organic search

### Remaining Opportunities
- Full SSR/prerendering (Rendertron)
- Image optimization (WebP/AVIF)
- Google Business Profile
- Blog content expansion (highest impact)
- Link building campaigns
- City-specific landing pages

## Notable Observations

- The SEO implementation is already quite mature — this is not a greenfield effort. Most foundational elements are in place.
- Blog content expansion is identified as the single most impactful SEO initiative, which aligns with the platform's vast domain expertise.
- The SPA indexing problem is the biggest technical risk — Rendertron or prerendering is critical.
- SA-specific keywords have relatively low competition, presenting a strong opportunity.
