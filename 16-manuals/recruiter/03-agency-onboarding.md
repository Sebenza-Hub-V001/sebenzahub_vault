# Chapter 3 — Agency onboarding: setting up your organisation

After you select **Recruiter** in Chapter 2, you land on the Agency Onboarding form. This is where Sebenza Hub creates your *organisation* — not just your user account. Get this right and the rest of the platform works the way your agency works. Get it wrong and you'll spend weeks fighting against badly-named records and misclassified sectors.

This chapter walks through each field, why it matters, and what gets created behind the scenes.

## What you're creating

Filling in this form creates three things at once:

1. **Your recruiter profile** — the personal record tied to your email. Holds your role, your link to the agency, your individual settings.
2. **Your agency organisation** — a separate "organisation" record that other team members can be invited into later. The agency is the entity that owns clients, jobs, candidates, and revenue.
3. **Your membership** — the link between you and the agency. Your first membership is automatically the **owner** of the agency, with full permissions.

This three-record structure is why you cannot later "convert" your account to a different agency. You can leave one agency and join another (covered in Chapter 7), but the records are distinct.

## Step 1 — Agency name (required)

The official name of your agency. Use the registered name if you have one (e.g. "Phakama Recruitment (Pty) Ltd"), not a trading shorthand. Clients and candidates will see this in offer letters and contracts.

You can change this later in Settings, but it's painful — historical placements, offers, and invoices keep the original name on the record.

## Step 2 — Website, email, phone

The contact details for your agency, not for you personally. These show up on:

- Your public agency Brand page (covered in Part 9)
- Your career site (covered in Part 9)
- Offer letters and email signatures
- The candidate-facing footer of every job listing

If your website isn't live yet, leave it blank — you can fill it in later. Don't put a placeholder like "coming soon"; an empty field is better than a dead link.

## Step 3 — Sectors (multi-select)

Pick the industries your agency recruits for. This is more important than it looks — sectors drive:

- **Trust tier categorisation** (covered in Chapter 6). Your reputation is calculated per sector, not globally. An agency that's Platinum in fintech but has no presence in mining will not appear as Platinum to a mining client.
- **Capacity Signal** (Chapter 6). Whether your agency shows as "Available", "Limited", or "At Capacity" is calculated per sector based on your active workload.
- **Competitive benchmarking** (Chapter 6). Your performance percentile is compared against peers in the same sectors, not a global recruiter pool.
- **Inbound matching.** When clients search for agencies, they filter by sector. If your sectors are wrong, you won't appear in the right searches.

Pick the sectors you actively recruit in *now*. You can add more later as your business expands. Don't pad the list — being "Platinum in 12 sectors" is less credible than being "Platinum in 3 sectors."

## Step 4 — Verification proof URL (optional)

A link to documents that prove your agency's legitimacy and compliance. Typical contents:

- Company registration document
- B-BBEE certificate (with rating and validity dates)
- POPIA compliance declaration
- Tax clearance certificate
- Professional indemnity insurance, if you carry it

This is **optional at signup** but **required for trust tier upgrades** (Chapter 6). You can skip it now and add it later, but you'll be stuck at Bronze trust tier until you provide proof.

The link should point to a document store (Google Drive, Dropbox, your own server). Set the link to "anyone with the link can view" — Sebenza Hub's verification team will need to access it. Don't put sensitive personal documents at this URL; only documents that establish the agency's identity and compliance status.

## Step 5 — Submit and land on your dashboard

Click **Complete onboarding**. The system:

1. Creates your recruiter profile.
2. Creates your agency organisation.
3. Creates your owner membership.
4. Sets your trust tier to **Bronze** (the default).
5. Redirects you to **/dashboard/recruiter/profile** — the next step is to fill out the rich agency profile, which Chapter 5 covers.

Don't worry if the form asks for things you haven't decided yet. The minimum to get past this screen is the agency name and at least one sector. Everything else can be edited from your profile page later.

## What happens next

You're now on the recruiter dashboard at `/dashboard/recruiter/profile`. Chapter 4 gives you a tour of what you're looking at — the dashboard layout, what each section means, and what to actually do in your first 30 minutes.

## Checklist

- [ ] You entered your agency's official registered name
- [ ] You picked the sectors you actively recruit in (not aspirational sectors)
- [ ] You either uploaded verification documents or noted that you'll need to before requesting a trust tier upgrade
- [ ] You landed on `/dashboard/recruiter/profile` after submitting
