# Sebenza Hub — Recruitment Features Guide

A comprehensive breakdown of every feature in the Sebenza Hub recruitment platform: what it is, how to use it, and why it matters.

---

## Recruitment

### Profile

**What it is:** Your recruiter profile — a central identity page displaying your details, expertise areas, placement history, and performance metrics.

**How we use it:** Recruiters complete their profile with specialisations, industries, and contact details. Clients and team members reference profiles to match the right recruiter to the right mandate.

**Why it's important:** A complete profile builds credibility, enables smart assignment of roles, and gives clients confidence in who is handling their search.

---

### Jobs

**What it is:** The job management hub where all open, paused, and closed requisitions live.

**How we use it:** Create new job listings, set requirements (skills, experience, location, salary band), assign hiring managers, and publish to job boards or the career site. Track each role from intake to fill.

**Why it's important:** A single source of truth for every role prevents duplicate effort, ensures nothing falls through the cracks, and gives leadership real-time visibility into the hiring pipeline.

---

### Pipeline

**What it is:** A visual, stage-based view of candidates moving through each job's hiring process — from applied to offered.

**How we use it:** Drag candidates between stages (Screening → Interview → Assessment → Offer), add notes, trigger automated actions at each stage, and monitor conversion rates.

**Why it's important:** Pipeline visibility prevents bottlenecks, highlights where candidates drop off, and keeps the entire team aligned on progress without status meetings.

---

### Screening Roles

**What it is:** Pre-configured screening criteria and workflows tied to specific role types.

**How we use it:** Define must-have qualifications, knockout questions, and minimum thresholds for each role category. When candidates apply, the system auto-screens against these criteria.

**Why it's important:** Standardised screening eliminates bias from initial filtering, saves hours of manual CV review, and ensures only qualified candidates reach recruiters.

---

### Candidates

**What it is:** The complete candidate database — every person who has ever applied, been sourced, or been referred.

**How we use it:** Search, filter, and segment candidates by skills, location, experience, availability, and previous interactions. View full profiles including CVs, assessment results, and communication history.

**Why it's important:** Your candidate database is your most valuable asset. Quick, intelligent access to it means faster fills, lower sourcing costs, and better candidate experiences.

---

### Talent CRM

**What it is:** A candidate relationship management system for nurturing talent over time, not just when a role is open.

**How we use it:** Tag candidates into segments, run drip email campaigns, track engagement, and set reminders for follow-ups. Build warm relationships before you need to make a placement.

**Why it's important:** The best candidates are often passive. A Talent CRM turns your database from a static list into a living, engaged community — reducing time-to-fill when roles open.

---

### AI Sourcing

**What it is:** An AI-powered tool that automatically identifies and recommends candidates from your database and connected sources who match a role's requirements.

**How we use it:** Open a job, activate AI Sourcing, and receive a ranked shortlist of matching candidates with match-score explanations. Review, adjust criteria, and reach out directly.

**Why it's important:** Manual sourcing is the biggest time sink in recruitment. AI Sourcing cuts sourcing time dramatically while surfacing candidates a human search might miss.

---

### Rediscovery

**What it is:** An intelligent feature that resurfaces previously overlooked or silver-medal candidates from your database who are now a fit for new roles.

**How we use it:** When a new role is created, Rediscovery automatically scans past applicants and shortlisted candidates from similar roles and flags strong matches.

**Why it's important:** Companies spend heavily on sourcing new candidates while sitting on goldmines of existing talent. Rediscovery maximises the ROI of every candidate you've ever engaged.

---

### LinkedIn Leads

**What it is:** Integration that imports and tracks leads generated from LinkedIn sourcing activity.

**How we use it:** Connect your LinkedIn activity, import prospect profiles, track outreach and response rates, and convert leads into candidates within the platform.

**Why it's important:** LinkedIn is the primary sourcing channel for most recruiters. Centralising this activity prevents data silos and ensures every interaction is tracked and measurable.

---

## Evaluation

### Compare

**What it is:** A side-by-side candidate comparison tool that evaluates multiple candidates against the same criteria.

**How we use it:** Select two or more candidates for a role and compare them across skills, experience, assessment scores, salary expectations, and cultural fit indicators.

**Why it's important:** Structured comparison removes gut-feel decision-making and gives hiring managers clear, defensible reasons for their choices.

---

### Scorecards

**What it is:** Standardised evaluation forms that interviewers complete after each candidate interaction.

**How we use it:** Create scorecards with role-specific criteria and rating scales. Interviewers fill them in immediately after interviews. Results aggregate into a single candidate score.

**Why it's important:** Scorecards ensure every interviewer evaluates on the same criteria, reduce bias, create a paper trail for compliance, and make debrief sessions faster and more productive.

---

### Offers

**What it is:** The central offer management module for creating, tracking, approving, and sending job offers.

**How we use it:** Generate offers from templates, route them through approval workflows, send them to candidates electronically, and track acceptance, negotiation, or rejection.

**Why it's important:** A slow or disorganised offer process loses candidates. Centralised offer management compresses time-to-offer and ensures nothing slips between HR, finance, and hiring managers.

---

### Offer Letters

**What it is:** Customisable, branded offer letter generation with dynamic fields.

**How we use it:** Select a template, auto-populate candidate and role details, customise terms, preview the letter, and send it for e-signature — all from within the platform.

**Why it's important:** Professional, error-free offer letters reflect your employer brand and reduce back-and-forth caused by manual document errors.

---

### Offer Analytics

**What it is:** Reporting on offer metrics — acceptance rates, time-to-accept, decline reasons, and negotiation frequency.

**How we use it:** Review dashboards showing offer performance by role, department, recruiter, and time period. Identify patterns in declines and adjust compensation strategies.

**Why it's important:** Understanding why offers succeed or fail lets you refine compensation packages, improve acceptance rates, and reduce costly re-hiring.

---

### Negotiations

**What it is:** A structured workspace for managing salary and benefits negotiations between candidates and hiring teams.

**How we use it:** Log negotiation rounds, track counter-offers, set approval limits, and maintain a full history of what was discussed and agreed.

**Why it's important:** Negotiation without structure leads to inconsistent packages, pay equity issues, and compliance risk. A clear record protects everyone.

---

### Offer Templates

**What it is:** Pre-built, reusable offer letter templates for different role types, levels, and regions.

**How we use it:** Create templates with standard clauses, benefits breakdowns, and conditional sections. Recruiters select the appropriate template and customise only what's needed.

**Why it's important:** Templates ensure legal consistency, speed up the offer process, and prevent critical clauses from being accidentally omitted.

---

### Offer Compare

**What it is:** A tool that lets candidates or hiring managers compare multiple offer scenarios side by side.

**How we use it:** Model different compensation packages (base vs. equity vs. benefits mix) and compare total value across scenarios to find the most competitive offer.

**Why it's important:** Candidates increasingly evaluate total compensation, not just salary. Offer Compare helps you present the strongest possible package.

---

### Offer Timeline

**What it is:** A visual timeline tracking every offer's journey from creation to acceptance or decline.

**How we use it:** Monitor where each offer stands, identify stalled offers, set SLA alerts, and ensure approvals don't bottleneck the process.

**Why it's important:** Visibility into offer timelines prevents candidates from going cold while waiting, directly protecting your offer acceptance rate.

---

### Offer Compliance

**What it is:** Automated compliance checks ensuring offers meet legal, policy, and equity requirements.

**How we use it:** The system validates offers against pay band policies, employment equity targets, B-BBEE requirements, and regional labour law before they can be sent.

**Why it's important:** Non-compliant offers create legal exposure. Automated checks catch issues before they reach candidates, protecting the organisation.

---

### Benchmarking

**What it is:** Compensation and market benchmarking data that informs competitive offer creation.

**How we use it:** When building an offer, pull benchmark data for the role, level, industry, and region to ensure your package is competitive. Compare internal offers against market rates.

**Why it's important:** Offers below market lose candidates; offers above market waste budget. Benchmarking finds the sweet spot.

---

### Offer Approvals

**What it is:** Configurable approval workflows that route offers to the right stakeholders before they're sent.

**How we use it:** Define approval chains by role level, salary threshold, or department. Approvers receive notifications, review offer details, and approve or request changes — all tracked.

**Why it's important:** Approval workflows enforce governance without creating email chains. They ensure the right people sign off while keeping the process moving.

---

### Tests

**What it is:** Skills and knowledge assessments that can be assigned to candidates at any pipeline stage.

**How we use it:** Assign pre-built or custom tests to candidates. Tests are completed online, auto-scored where possible, and results attach to the candidate profile.

**Why it's important:** Objective testing validates what a CV claims, reduces mis-hires, and gives candidates a fair chance to demonstrate ability beyond their interview performance.

---

### Test Library

**What it is:** A repository of ready-made assessments across technical skills, cognitive ability, personality, and role-specific knowledge.

**How we use it:** Browse the library by category, preview questions, and assign tests to candidates or attach them to specific pipeline stages for automatic delivery.

**Why it's important:** A rich test library means you can assess candidates immediately without spending time creating assessments from scratch.

---

### Test Templates

**What it is:** Customisable assessment templates that let you build your own tests using a mix of question types.

**How we use it:** Create templates with multiple-choice, free-text, coding challenges, or scenario-based questions. Save and reuse them across similar roles.

**Why it's important:** Templates let you assess for company-specific skills and values that off-the-shelf tests don't cover.

---

### Interview Kits

**What it is:** Structured interview packages containing questions, evaluation criteria, and guidance for each interview stage.

**How we use it:** Create kits for each role type and interview round. Interviewers receive the kit before the meeting, follow the structured questions, and score against defined criteria.

**Why it's important:** Interview kits ensure consistency across interviewers, reduce the impact of individual bias, and make it easy for new interviewers to conduct effective assessments.

---

### Video Interviews

**What it is:** Integrated video interviewing — both live and asynchronous (one-way recorded) — within the platform.

**How we use it:** Schedule live video interviews or send candidates a set of questions to record their responses at their convenience. Review recordings, share with the hiring team, and score.

**Why it's important:** Video interviews expand your reach to remote candidates, enable asynchronous collaboration across time zones, and create a reviewable record of each interaction.

---

### Background Checks

**What it is:** Integrated background verification covering criminal records, employment history, qualifications, and credit checks.

**How we use it:** Trigger background checks from the candidate profile or at a specific pipeline stage. Results are returned to the platform and flagged if issues are found.

**Why it's important:** Background checks protect the organisation from negligent hiring claims and verify candidate honesty — essential for trust-sensitive roles.

---

### Blind Hiring

**What it is:** A feature that anonymises candidate information (name, photo, age, gender, institution) during screening and evaluation stages.

**How we use it:** Enable blind hiring on a per-role basis. Reviewers see skills, experience, and assessment results without identifying information until later stages.

**Why it's important:** Blind hiring directly reduces unconscious bias, supports diversity goals, and ensures candidates are evaluated on merit.

---

### AI Intelligence

**What it is:** AI-powered insights and recommendations layered across the evaluation process.

**How we use it:** The system surfaces pattern-based insights — flagging at-risk candidates, predicting offer acceptance likelihood, identifying skill gaps, and recommending next-best actions.

**Why it's important:** AI Intelligence turns raw hiring data into actionable guidance, helping recruiters make faster, smarter decisions.

---

### AI Candidate Summary

**What it is:** AI-generated candidate summaries that distil lengthy CVs and profiles into concise overviews with key strengths, risk assessments, fit analysis, interview recommendations, and role requirement comparisons.

**How we use it:** Open any candidate profile and view the AI-generated summary. Use it to quickly assess fit, identify talking points for interviews, and compare candidates without reading every page of every CV.

**Why it's important:** Reviewing a 5-page CV takes 7 minutes. An AI summary takes 10 seconds to read and highlights exactly what matters for the specific role. This accelerates recruiter judgment, allowing more candidates to be reviewed with the same level of attention.

---

### Manager Portal

**What it is:** A dedicated, simplified interface for hiring managers to participate in the recruitment process without needing full platform access.

**How we use it:** Hiring managers log in to review candidates, complete scorecards, approve offers, provide feedback, and track their roles' progress — all through a streamlined view.

**Why it's important:** Hiring manager engagement is the single biggest factor in recruitment speed. A purpose-built portal removes friction and keeps managers actively involved.

## AI Tools

### AI JD Writer

**What it is:** An AI tool that generates compelling, inclusive job descriptions from minimal input.

**How we use it:** Enter the role title, key requirements, and team context. The AI produces a complete job description with responsibilities, requirements, benefits, and inclusive language suggestions.

**Why it's important:** Great job descriptions attract more and better applicants. AI JD Writer ensures every listing is professional, complete, and free of exclusionary language.

---

### AI Questions

**What it is:** AI-generated interview and screening questions tailored to a specific role and seniority level.

**How we use it:** Select a role and the system generates behavioural, technical, and situational questions calibrated to the position. Customise as needed and add them to interview kits.

**Why it's important:** Quality questions are the foundation of quality interviews. AI Questions ensure you're always asking the right things, even for unfamiliar roles.

---

### AI Search

**What it is:** Natural-language search across your entire candidate database.

**How we use it:** Type a plain-English query like "senior Python developer in Cape Town with fintech experience, open to contract" and receive ranked results.

**Why it's important:** Boolean search is powerful but slow and error-prone. AI Search makes your full database accessible to every team member, regardless of search expertise.

---

### Screening Bot

**What it is:** An AI-powered chatbot that conducts initial candidate screening conversations automatically.

**How we use it:** Configure screening criteria for a role. The bot engages applicants via chat, asks qualifying questions, collects responses, and scores candidates — passing qualified ones to the recruiter.

**Why it's important:** The Screening Bot handles high-volume initial screening 24/7, ensuring every applicant gets a timely response while freeing recruiters for high-value activities.

---

### Predictive Hiring

**What it is:** Machine learning models that predict hiring outcomes — likelihood of offer acceptance, expected tenure, and performance potential.

**How we use it:** View predictive scores on candidate profiles and in pipeline views. Use predictions to prioritise outreach, adjust offers, and focus effort where it's most likely to pay off.

**Why it's important:** Predictive Hiring shifts recruitment from reactive to proactive, reducing time-to-fill and improving quality of hire through data-driven decisions.

---

### Culture Fit Intel

**What it is:** AI analysis that assesses alignment between a candidate's values, working style, and motivations and the hiring team's culture.

**How we use it:** Candidates complete a short assessment. The system compares their profile against team data and provides a compatibility report with specific talking points for interviews.

**Why it's important:** Culture misalignment is a leading cause of early attrition. Culture Fit Intel surfaces potential friction before an offer is made.

---

### Video Analysis

**What it is:** AI analysis of recorded video interviews that provides insights on communication style, confidence, and engagement.

**How we use it:** After a video interview is recorded, the system analyses verbal and non-verbal cues and generates a summary report. Reviewers use this alongside their own assessments.

**Why it's important:** Video Analysis adds a structured, objective layer to a naturally subjective process, helping identify strong communicators and flagging potential concerns.

## Talent & Engagement

### Referrals

**What it is:** An employee and network referral management system that tracks referrals from submission to hire.

**How we use it:** Share referral links, track who referred whom, manage referral bonuses, and measure referral programme effectiveness by source, department, and outcome.

**Why it's important:** Referred candidates hire faster, stay longer, and perform better. A structured referral system turns your network into your best sourcing channel.

---

### Candidate NPS

**What it is:** Net Promoter Score surveys sent to candidates at key points in their recruitment journey.

**How we use it:** Automated NPS surveys go out after application, interview, and offer stages. Track scores over time, by recruiter, and by role. Identify and act on negative experiences.

**Why it's important:** Candidate experience directly impacts employer brand. NPS gives you a quantifiable measure to track and improve it.

---

### Onboarding

**What it is:** A structured onboarding workflow that bridges the gap between offer acceptance and productive first day.

**How we use it:** Create onboarding checklists, assign tasks to HR/IT/managers, send welcome materials, collect documents, and track completion — all before day one.

**Why it's important:** Poor onboarding drives early attrition. Structured onboarding ensures new hires feel welcome, prepared, and productive from day one.

---

### Talent Pools

**What it is:** Curated groups of candidates segmented by skill, role type, location, or other criteria for future opportunities.

**How we use it:** Add candidates to talent pools manually or automatically based on tags and filters. When a new role opens, check relevant pools first before sourcing externally.

**Why it's important:** Talent Pools turn every recruitment effort into a long-term investment. Even candidates not hired today become a ready pipeline for tomorrow.

---

### Re-Engagement

**What it is:** Automated campaigns to re-activate dormant candidates in your database.

**How we use it:** Set criteria for dormancy (e.g., no interaction in 6 months), create re-engagement email sequences, and track who re-engages. Updated candidates re-enter active talent pools.

**Why it's important:** Databases decay quickly as candidates change roles and update skills. Re-Engagement keeps your data fresh and your pipeline warm.

---

### Reference Checks

**What it is:** Automated reference checking that collects and verifies candidate references digitally.

**How we use it:** Send reference request forms to referees, track completion, receive structured feedback, and flag inconsistencies — all without manual phone calls.

**Why it's important:** Manual reference checks are time-consuming and often skipped. Automation ensures they happen consistently while saving recruiter time.

## Analytics

### Overview

**What it is:** A high-level analytics dashboard showing key recruitment metrics at a glance.

**How we use it:** View open roles, active candidates, time-to-fill, offer acceptance rates, and pipeline health in a single dashboard. Filter by team, time period, or client.

**Why it's important:** Leadership needs quick, accurate visibility into recruitment performance. The overview dashboard delivers this without requiring custom reports.

---

### Predictive

**What it is:** Forward-looking analytics that forecast hiring outcomes based on historical data and current pipeline activity.

**How we use it:** View predictions for time-to-fill, likely bottlenecks, and projected hires per period. Use forecasts to allocate resources and set realistic expectations with stakeholders.

**Why it's important:** Reactive reporting tells you what happened. Predictive analytics tells you what's about to happen — giving you time to act.

---

### Team DNA

**What it is:** Analytics on team composition, working styles, and skill distributions within hiring teams.

**How we use it:** View team profiles showing strengths, gaps, and diversity metrics. Use insights to guide hiring decisions that complement existing team capabilities.

**Why it's important:** Hiring for team balance, not just individual skills, leads to higher-performing, more resilient teams.

---

### Panel Analysis

**What it is:** Analytics on interview panel effectiveness — how individual interviewers score, their consistency, and potential bias patterns.

**How we use it:** Review interviewer scorecards, identify calibration issues, spot outliers, and coach interviewers who consistently over- or under-rate candidates.

**Why it's important:** Uncalibrated panels make unpredictable decisions. Panel Analysis ensures your interview process is fair, consistent, and improving over time.

---

### Engagement Score

**What it is:** A composite score measuring candidate engagement throughout the recruitment process.

**How we use it:** View engagement scores on candidate profiles — based on email opens, response times, assessment completion, and interview attendance. Prioritise highly engaged candidates.

**Why it's important:** Engagement is the strongest predictor of offer acceptance. Tracking it lets you invest time where it's most likely to convert.

---

### Diversity Analytics

**What it is:** Comprehensive reporting on diversity metrics across the entire recruitment funnel.

**How we use it:** Track demographic representation at each pipeline stage — from application to hire. Identify where diverse candidates drop off and take corrective action.

**Why it's important:** You can't improve what you don't measure. Diversity Analytics provides the data needed to build genuinely inclusive hiring processes.

---

### Requirement Check

**What it is:** An automated audit that verifies whether a role's requirements are being met by the current candidate pipeline.

**How we use it:** The system compares role requirements against the active pipeline and flags gaps — e.g., "No candidates meet the SQL requirement" or "All shortlisted candidates exceed budget."

**Why it's important:** Early identification of pipeline-role mismatches saves time and prevents last-minute scrambles when shortlists don't meet hiring manager expectations.

---

### Offer Predictor

**What it is:** An AI model that predicts the likelihood of a candidate accepting an offer based on historical patterns and current signals.

**How we use it:** Before making an offer, check the prediction score. The system considers factors like candidate engagement, competing offers, salary expectations, and time-in-process.

**Why it's important:** Making offers that get declined wastes time and budget. Offer Predictor helps you refine packages and timing to maximise acceptance.

### B-BBEE

**What it is:** Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment tracking and reporting tools specific to South African compliance requirements.

**How we use it:** Track B-BBEE status of candidates and placements, generate compliance reports, and monitor progress against transformation targets.

**Why it's important:** B-BBEE compliance is a legal requirement in South Africa and affects tender eligibility. Accurate tracking ensures compliance and supports transformation goals.

### Employment Equity

**What it is:** Employment Equity Act compliance tracking for South African workforce demographics and reporting.

**How we use it:** Monitor hiring against EE targets by occupational level, track demographic representation, and generate Department of Labour reports.

**Why it's important:** EE compliance is legally mandated. The platform ensures accurate reporting and helps organisations meet their equity targets through data-driven hiring.

### POPIA Compliance

**What it is:** Protection of Personal Information Act compliance tools ensuring candidate data is handled lawfully.

**How we use it:** Manage consent records, enforce data retention policies, handle data subject access requests, and ensure candidate data is collected, stored, and shared in compliance with POPIA.

**Why it's important:** POPIA non-compliance carries severe penalties. Built-in compliance tools protect both candidates' privacy rights and the organisation's legal standing.
