# Sebenza Hub — Individual Features Guide

A comprehensive breakdown of every feature available to individual users (job seekers and professionals) on the Sebenza Hub platform: what it is, how to use it, and why it matters.

---

## Profile

### My CVs

**What it is:** A personal CV management hub where you can create, store, and manage multiple versions of your CV.

**How we use it:** Upload existing CVs or build new ones from scratch. Maintain different versions tailored to different industries or role types. Select which CV to attach when applying for jobs.

**Why it's important:** Different roles require different emphasis. Having multiple CV versions ready means you can apply quickly with the most relevant version, increasing your chances of getting shortlisted.

---

### Credentials

**What it is:** A verified digital wallet for your qualifications, certifications, licences, and professional memberships.

**How we use it:** Upload credential documents, link to verification sources, and set expiry reminders. Verified credentials display a trust badge on your profile.

**Why it's important:** Verified credentials build instant trust with recruiters and employers. They eliminate back-and-forth document requests and fast-track you through screening stages.

---

### Video Profile

**What it is:** A short recorded video introduction that supplements your written CV and lets employers see and hear you before an interview.

**How we use it:** Record a 60–90 second video introducing yourself — your background, strengths, and what you're looking for. The video attaches to your profile and is visible to recruiters.

**Why it's important:** A video profile humanises your application and helps you stand out in a sea of text-based CVs. It showcases communication skills, confidence, and personality that a CV can't convey.

---

### Skill Verifications

**What it is:** Validated skill assessments that prove your claimed abilities through standardised tests.

**How we use it:** Take skill verification tests in your areas of expertise. Passing a test adds a verified badge to that skill on your profile, visible to all recruiters.

**Why it's important:** Anyone can list skills on a CV. Verified skills give you a competitive edge by providing objective proof of your abilities, making recruiters more likely to shortlist you.

---

### CV Templates

**What it is:** A library of professionally designed CV templates that you can use to build polished, well-structured CVs.

**How we use it:** Browse templates by industry or style, select one, and fill in your details. The template handles formatting, layout, and design so you can focus on content.

**Why it's important:** Presentation matters. A well-formatted CV makes a strong first impression, is easier for recruiters to scan, and ensures your content is structured in the way hiring managers expect.

---

## Job Search

### All Jobs

**What it is:** The complete job listings board showing all available opportunities on the platform.

**How we use it:** Browse, search, and filter jobs by title, industry, location, salary range, job type, and experience level. Click into any listing for full details and apply directly.

**Why it's important:** A centralised job board gives you access to all opportunities in one place, saving time compared to checking multiple sources.

---

### Auto Match

**What it is:** An AI-powered feature that automatically matches you with jobs based on your profile, skills, experience, and preferences.

**How we use it:** Complete your profile and set your job preferences. The system continuously scans new listings and notifies you when a strong match appears — no manual searching needed.

**Why it's important:** You can't search 24/7, but Auto Match can. It ensures you never miss a relevant opportunity, including roles you might not have found through manual search.

---

### Manual Search

**What it is:** An advanced search tool with detailed filters for finding specific job opportunities.

**How we use it:** Use keyword search combined with filters — location, salary, industry, company size, remote/hybrid/onsite, contract type — to narrow down exactly the roles you want.

**Why it's important:** When you know exactly what you're looking for, Manual Search gives you precision control to find it quickly without wading through irrelevant listings.

---

### Saved Searches

**What it is:** The ability to save your search criteria and receive alerts when new jobs match those criteria.

**How we use it:** After setting your filters in Manual Search, save the search with a name. You'll receive notifications whenever new jobs matching those criteria are posted.

**Why it's important:** Saved Searches automate your job hunt. Instead of repeating the same search daily, you get proactive alerts — keeping you among the first to apply.

---

### Favourites

**What it is:** A personal shortlist of jobs you're interested in but haven't applied to yet.

**How we use it:** Click the favourite icon on any job listing to save it. Review your favourites list when you're ready to apply, compare options, or revisit roles you're considering.

**Why it's important:** Job searching is a process of comparison. Favourites let you bookmark and come back to opportunities without losing track of them.

---

### Company Research

**What it is:** Detailed company profiles providing insights into potential employers — culture, size, industry, reviews, and hiring activity.

**How we use it:** Before applying, research the company's profile to understand their values, team size, growth trajectory, and what current and former employees say about working there.

**Why it's important:** Applying blindly wastes time. Company Research helps you target employers that align with your values and career goals, and prepares you for interviews with informed questions.

---

## Applications

### My Applications

**What it is:** A personal dashboard showing every job you've applied for, with current status and history.

**How we use it:** View all your applications in one place, see which stage each is at (submitted, reviewed, interview, offer), and access application details and correspondence.

**Why it's important:** When you're applying to multiple roles, it's easy to lose track. My Applications keeps everything organised and ensures you never miss a follow-up.

---

### App Tracker

**What it is:** A visual pipeline tracker that shows the progress of each application through the hiring stages.

**How we use it:** See a timeline view of each application — when you applied, when it was viewed, when you moved to the next stage. Get notified of status changes in real time.

**Why it's important:** Transparency reduces anxiety. Knowing where you stand lets you plan your next moves, follow up at the right time, and focus energy on active opportunities.

---

### App Analytics

**What it is:** Personal analytics on your application performance — response rates, stage conversion, and trends.

**How we use it:** Review metrics like how many applications resulted in interviews, which industries respond most, and how your success rate compares over time. Identify patterns and adjust your strategy.

**Why it's important:** Data-driven job searching is more effective. App Analytics shows you what's working and what isn't, so you can refine your approach instead of repeating the same mistakes.

---

### Notes & Tags

**What it is:** A personal annotation system for adding notes and tags to your job applications.

**How we use it:** Add notes to any application — reminders about the role, interview prep points, or contact details. Tag applications by priority, industry, or any custom label.

**Why it's important:** Staying organised across dozens of applications is crucial. Notes and tags give you a personal CRM for your job search, keeping important details at your fingertips.

---

### Reminders

**What it is:** Customisable alerts and reminders tied to your applications and job search activities.

**How we use it:** Set reminders to follow up on an application, prepare for an upcoming interview, submit requested documents, or check back on a saved job.

**Why it's important:** Missing a deadline or forgetting to follow up can cost you an opportunity. Reminders ensure you stay on top of every action item in your job search.

---

### Application Portal

**What it is:** A unified portal where you manage all incoming requests from employers — document submissions, additional questions, and pre-screening forms.

**How we use it:** When an employer requests additional information, it appears in your Application Portal. Complete and submit everything from one place without switching between emails.

**Why it's important:** A centralised portal prevents missed requests and ensures you respond promptly, signalling professionalism and genuine interest to employers.

---

### Candidate Hub

**What it is:** Your personal dashboard — a central landing page aggregating your profile status, active applications, upcoming interviews, and recommended actions.

**How we use it:** Start your day at the Candidate Hub. See at a glance what needs attention: pending applications, interview prep, messages from recruiters, and new job matches.

**Why it's important:** The Candidate Hub eliminates the need to check multiple sections. It surfaces what matters most right now, keeping you focused and productive.

---

### Interview Scheduling

**What it is:** A self-service tool for viewing and booking available interview time slots offered by employers.

**How we use it:** When invited to interview, view available slots, select the time that works best for you, and confirm. The interview is added to your calendar automatically.

**Why it's important:** Self-scheduling puts you in control, eliminates email back-and-forth, and lets you choose times when you'll perform your best.

---

### Experience Surveys

**What it is:** Short surveys sent to you at key points in the recruitment process to capture your experience.

**How we use it:** Complete brief surveys after applying, interviewing, or receiving an outcome. Your feedback is anonymous and helps improve the recruitment process.

**Why it's important:** Your feedback shapes how employers recruit. Experience Surveys give you a voice and help ensure future candidates have a better experience.

---

### Messages

**What it is:** An in-platform messaging system for communicating directly with recruiters and employers.

**How we use it:** Send and receive messages, ask questions about roles, respond to recruiter outreach, and keep all recruitment-related conversations in one threaded view.

**Why it's important:** Centralised messaging ensures no communication gets lost in email spam folders and gives you a complete history of every conversation.

---

## AI Tools

### AI Coaching

**What it is:** An AI-powered career coach that provides personalised guidance on your job search strategy, interview preparation, and career decisions.

**How we use it:** Chat with the AI coach about your career goals, current challenges, or upcoming interviews. Receive tailored advice, practice questions, and actionable next steps.

**Why it's important:** Not everyone has access to a career coach. AI Coaching provides on-demand, personalised guidance that helps you make smarter career moves and present yourself more effectively.

---

### CV Review

**What it is:** An AI tool that analyses your CV and provides specific, actionable feedback on content, structure, and impact.

**How we use it:** Upload your CV and receive a detailed review covering clarity, keyword optimisation, achievement quantification, formatting issues, and gaps that recruiters will notice.

**Why it's important:** Most CVs are rejected in seconds. AI CV Review helps you fix the issues that cause rejection before you apply, dramatically improving your shortlist rate.

---

### Cover Letter

**What it is:** An AI-powered cover letter generator that creates tailored cover letters for specific job applications.

**How we use it:** Select a job you're applying for, and the AI generates a cover letter that aligns your experience with the role's requirements. Edit, personalise, and submit.

**Why it's important:** A generic cover letter hurts more than it helps. AI-generated, role-specific cover letters save time while ensuring each application feels personalised and relevant.

---

### Salary Negotiator

**What it is:** An AI tool that helps you prepare for salary negotiations with data-driven insights and conversation strategies.

**How we use it:** Enter the role, your experience level, and location. The tool provides market salary data, suggests negotiation talking points, and helps you rehearse the conversation.

**Why it's important:** Most people leave money on the table because they don't negotiate or negotiate poorly. The Salary Negotiator gives you the data and confidence to advocate for fair compensation.

---

## Learning Hub

### Learning Dashboard

**What it is:** Your personal learning home page showing enrolled courses, progress, achievements, and recommended next steps.

**How we use it:** Track your learning progress across all active courses and paths. See completion percentages, upcoming deadlines, and personalised recommendations based on your career goals.

**Why it's important:** A clear dashboard keeps you motivated and on track. Seeing your progress builds momentum and helps you prioritise what to learn next.

---

### Learning Paths

**What it is:** Curated sequences of courses and activities designed to build competency in a specific skill or role.

**How we use it:** Browse learning paths by career goal — e.g., "Become a Data Analyst" or "Master Project Management." Enrol and work through the structured sequence at your own pace.

**Why it's important:** Random courses don't build careers. Learning Paths provide a structured roadmap that takes you from where you are to where you want to be, step by step.

---

### Skill Tree

**What it is:** A visual map of your skills showing current levels, relationships between skills, and growth opportunities.

**How we use it:** View your skill tree to understand your strengths and gaps. Click on any skill to see learning resources, verification tests, and related career paths.

**Why it's important:** The Skill Tree gives you a clear picture of your professional capability and shows you exactly where to invest your learning time for maximum career impact.

---

### Micro Learning

**What it is:** Short, focused learning modules (5–15 minutes) that teach a single concept or skill.

**How we use it:** Complete bite-sized lessons during commutes, lunch breaks, or between tasks. Each module covers one topic and includes a quick knowledge check.

**Why it's important:** Not everyone has hours to dedicate to learning. Micro Learning makes skill development accessible in small, consistent doses — and consistency beats intensity.

---

### Interview Simulator

**What it is:** An AI-powered mock interview tool that simulates real interview scenarios and provides feedback on your responses.

**How we use it:** Select an interview type (behavioural, technical, case study) and role. The simulator asks questions, you respond (text or video), and the AI provides detailed feedback on content, structure, and delivery.

**Why it's important:** Interview skills improve with practice, but practice opportunities are rare. The simulator gives you unlimited, risk-free practice with actionable feedback.

---

### Job-Linked Learning

**What it is:** Learning recommendations directly tied to the jobs you're interested in or have applied for.

**How we use it:** When viewing a job listing, see which skills you're missing and access targeted learning modules to close those gaps before or during the application process.

**Why it's important:** Job-Linked Learning turns a "not quite qualified" into "qualified." It shows you exactly what to learn to become competitive for the roles you want.

---

### Challenges

**What it is:** Practical, project-based challenges that test and develop your skills through real-world scenarios.

**How we use it:** Browse available challenges by skill area, complete them within the given timeframe, and submit your work. Top submissions earn recognition and badges.

**Why it's important:** Challenges prove you can apply knowledge, not just memorise it. Completed challenges become portfolio pieces and demonstrate initiative to employers.

---

### Portfolio Builder

**What it is:** A tool for creating and showcasing a professional portfolio of your work, projects, and achievements.

**How we use it:** Add projects, challenge submissions, code samples, designs, or case studies. Organise them by skill or industry and share your portfolio link with employers.

**Why it's important:** A portfolio shows, not tells. For many roles, seeing your actual work is more persuasive than any CV bullet point.

---

### Peer Teaching

**What it is:** A collaborative learning feature where you can teach topics you're strong in and learn from others.

**How we use it:** Offer to teach skills you've mastered and sign up for sessions taught by peers. Teaching earns you recognition and badges while reinforcing your own knowledge.

**Why it's important:** Teaching is the deepest form of learning. Peer Teaching builds your expertise, communication skills, and professional network simultaneously.

---

### SETA Learnerships

**What it is:** Access to Sector Education and Training Authority (SETA) accredited learnership programmes in South Africa.

**How we use it:** Browse available learnerships by SETA and industry, check eligibility requirements, and apply directly through the platform.

**Why it's important:** SETA learnerships provide structured, accredited training with practical workplace experience — a proven pathway into employment, especially for early-career individuals.

---

### Certifications

**What it is:** A catalogue of professional certifications you can pursue, with study resources and exam preparation support.

**How we use it:** Browse certifications relevant to your career goals, access preparation materials and study guides, and track your progress toward certification readiness.

**Why it's important:** Certifications validate your expertise to employers and often unlock higher salary bands and more senior roles. They're especially valuable in regulated industries.

---

### Study Planner

**What it is:** A personal study scheduling tool that helps you plan and stick to your learning commitments.

**How we use it:** Set your learning goals and available study time. The planner creates a realistic schedule, sends reminders, and adjusts if you fall behind or get ahead.

**Why it's important:** Good intentions without a plan rarely lead to results. The Study Planner turns "I should learn that" into a concrete, achievable schedule.

---

### Learning Analytics

**What it is:** Detailed analytics on your learning activity — time spent, modules completed, quiz scores, and skill growth over time.

**How we use it:** Review your learning patterns, identify which topics you grasp quickly and which need more attention, and track how your skill profile has evolved.

**Why it's important:** Learning Analytics shows you the return on your time investment and helps you learn more effectively by understanding your own patterns.

---

### Mentorship

**What it is:** A structured mentorship programme connecting you with experienced professionals in your field.

**How we use it:** Browse available mentors by industry and expertise, request a connection, and schedule mentorship sessions. Track goals and progress within the mentorship relationship.

**Why it's important:** A good mentor accelerates your career by years. Structured mentorship provides guidance, accountability, and access to networks you wouldn't reach alone.

---

## Career Development

### Skill Gap

**What it is:** An analysis tool that identifies the gaps between your current skills and the requirements of your target roles.

**How we use it:** Select your target role or career path. The tool compares your verified skills against role requirements and produces a prioritised list of gaps with linked learning resources.

**Why it's important:** Knowing what you lack is the first step to closing the gap. Skill Gap analysis gives you a clear, actionable development plan instead of vague career anxiety.

---

### Career Path

**What it is:** A visual career progression tool showing potential career trajectories based on your current skills and experience.

**How we use it:** Explore possible career paths, see what roles you could move into, what skills each transition requires, and what the typical timeline looks like. Plan lateral moves, promotions, or career pivots.

**Why it's important:** Career decisions made without data are risky. Career Path gives you a map of where you can go and what it takes to get there.

---

### Salary Insights

**What it is:** Market salary data showing compensation ranges for roles by title, industry, experience level, and location.

**How we use it:** Search for a role and see salary ranges — median, 25th percentile, and 75th percentile. Compare across locations and industries to understand your market value.

**Why it's important:** Knowing your market value empowers you in job searches and negotiations. Salary Insights ensures you're neither underselling yourself nor pricing yourself out of opportunities.

---

### Career Pulse

**What it is:** A periodic check-in tool that assesses your career satisfaction, progress, and alignment with your goals.

**How we use it:** Complete regular Career Pulse surveys. The tool tracks your sentiment over time and flags when you might be ready for a change, need development, or should explore new opportunities.

**Why it's important:** Career drift happens slowly. Career Pulse helps you stay intentional about your professional life by surfacing dissatisfaction or misalignment before it becomes a crisis.

---

### Tests

**What it is:** Skills and knowledge assessments that validate your abilities and add verified credentials to your profile.

**How we use it:** Browse available tests by category, take assessments online, and receive immediate results. Passing scores add verified badges to your profile.

**Why it's important:** Test results provide objective evidence of your capabilities, giving you an advantage over candidates who only claim skills on their CV.

---

### Analytics

**What it is:** A personal career analytics dashboard showing your profile strength, market competitiveness, and job search performance.

**How we use it:** Review metrics including profile views, search appearances, application success rates, and how you compare to other candidates in your field.

**Why it's important:** Analytics turn your career management from guesswork into strategy. Understanding how the market sees you helps you make targeted improvements.

---

## Community

### Community Forum

**What it is:** A discussion forum where job seekers, professionals, and mentors share advice, experiences, and support.

**How we use it:** Browse topics, ask questions, share your experiences, and engage with others in your industry or career stage. Participate in discussions to build your network and reputation.

**Why it's important:** Job searching can be isolating. The Community Forum provides peer support, practical advice, and connections that make the journey less lonely and more effective.

---

### Trending

**What it is:** A curated feed of trending topics, discussions, and content within the community.

**How we use it:** Check Trending to see what the community is talking about — hot industries, common interview questions, salary trends, and career advice that's resonating.

**Why it's important:** Staying current on what matters to your peers and the market keeps you informed and helps you spot opportunities and trends early.

---

### Leaderboard

**What it is:** A gamified ranking system that recognises active community members based on contributions, learning, and engagement.

**How we use it:** Earn points by completing courses, helping peers, taking assessments, and engaging in the community. See where you rank and strive to climb the leaderboard.

**Why it's important:** The Leaderboard motivates consistent engagement and learning. High-ranking members build visibility and credibility that can attract recruiter attention.

---

### Interview Vault

**What it is:** A shared repository of real interview questions and experiences contributed by community members.

**How we use it:** Search by company or role type to find interview questions others have encountered. Contribute your own experiences after interviews to help the community.

**Why it's important:** Knowing what to expect in an interview is a massive advantage. The Interview Vault gives you insider knowledge that would otherwise require expensive coaching or lucky connections.

---

### Mentor Connect

**What it is:** A matchmaking feature that connects you with mentors based on your career goals, industry, and development needs.

**How we use it:** Complete your mentorship profile with your goals and preferences. The system suggests compatible mentors. Send connection requests and schedule your first session.

**Why it's important:** Finding the right mentor is hard. Mentor Connect removes the guesswork and connects you with people who have walked the path you're trying to follow.

---

### Badges

**What it is:** Digital achievement badges earned through learning, assessments, community contributions, and career milestones.

**How we use it:** Earn badges by completing courses, passing skill verifications, helping peers, and reaching career milestones. Badges display on your profile and are visible to recruiters.

**Why it's important:** Badges are micro-credentials that signal commitment and growth. They make your profile more attractive and provide conversation starters with recruiters.

---

## Account

### Billing

**What it is:** Subscription and payment management for your Sebenza Hub individual account.

**How we use it:** View your current plan, upgrade for premium features, manage payment methods, download invoices, and track your subscription status.

**Why it's important:** Clear billing management ensures you have access to the features you need and prevents unexpected charges.

---

### Settings

**What it is:** Personal account configuration including privacy, notifications, integrations, and profile visibility.

**How we use it:** Control who can see your profile, manage email and push notification preferences, connect external accounts (LinkedIn, GitHub), and configure your job search preferences.

**Why it's important:** Proper settings ensure the platform works for you — protecting your privacy when needed, alerting you to what matters, and keeping irrelevant noise out of your experience.
